Research

Previous research observed the bimodal cloud-field alignment for the molecular cloud in Gould Belt (Li, 2013). Which indicated that molecular clouds in the Gould Belt are either perpendicular or parallel to the mean magnetic field direction when projected into the plane of the sky. The mass cumulative function slope (M) shows a significant correlation with the cloud-field offset (Law, 2020). As astronomical observation loses the depth of the cloud due to the projection effect, it is difficult to compare the 3D alignment of molecular cloud and magnetic field from observation. Therefore, our study aimed to analyze the relation between M and cloud-field offset with simulated clouds with known intrinsic 3D cloud-field alignment.
